The Effect of Semantic and Visual Feedforward on Online Form Filling Tasks Sep 2021 - Dec 2021
• To bridge the potential digital divide in the completion of common online form tasks by specific populations, 113 static HTML/CSS forms with different combinations of semantic and visual feedforwards were generated to study the impact of different feedforwards on task completion.
• Carried out six consecutive experiments on the Python website built with the Django framework and deployed on AWS. The quantitative experimental data were collected through a 7-point Likert scale questionnaire, including user preferences, difficulty perception, and semantic understanding, etc. These data are stored in in a SQLite database.
• Completed data processing and model analysis through SPSS to form feedforward design guidelines. The validity and practicability of the guidelines were verified through interviews with practitioners in the field of HCI design.

Sentiment Analysis for Reddit Based on Machine Learning and Feature Extraction Jun 2021 - Aug 2021
• Performed data preprocessing and feature extraction on 88,489 high-density noisy subreddit texts using Scikit-learn, SpaCy, NLTK, and Gensim, and text weights were computed via one-hot encoding and TF-IDF values.
• Compared the precision, recall, and F1-score of different machine learning models (Logistic Regression, SVM, Bernoulli Naive Bayes, LDA and self-built Pipeline), and fine-tuned the parameters to achieve better results. Visualized experimental results using the Matplotlib library.
